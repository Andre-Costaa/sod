Content Architecture & Structure

üìê Structural Analysis: Hub & Spoke Model Assessment

Current State

The site employs a "Hub & Spoke" content architecture where main topic pages (hubs) link to supporting subtopic pages (spokes). However, analysis reveals that:

  • Hub pages (25 pages) average 1,200 words ‚Äî adequate depth
  • Spoke pages (632 pages) average only 240 words ‚Äî critically thin
  • Orphan pages (78 pages) lack hub connections ‚Äî poor internal linking

Recommended Structure

To maximize SEO value and user engagement:

  • Hub pages: Expand to 1,500-3,000 words with comprehensive topic coverage
  • Spoke pages: Minimum 800 words with detailed explanations, examples, use cases
  • Internal linking: Implement bidirectional linking between hubs and spokes
  • Topic clusters: Group related spokes under thematic hubs

Implementation Priority

  1. Phase 1: Expand the 187 pages with 0-100 words (highest priority, likely de-indexed)
  2. Phase 2: Expand the 445 pages with 101-300 words (at-risk category)
  3. Phase 3: Strengthen the 25 hub pages with additional depth (1,500-2,000 words)
  4. Phase 4: Connect orphan pages to relevant hubs via strategic internal links

Content Expansion Framework

For Each Thin Content Page:

  1. Add detailed definition/explanation section (200-300 words)
  2. Include 2-3 practical examples or use cases (150-250 words)
  3. Add "Common Questions" FAQ section (200-300 words)

  5. Add 5-8 strategic internal links to related pages
  6. Include multimedia (images, diagrams, videos) where applicable
  7. Add "Related Topics" or "Further Reading" section
